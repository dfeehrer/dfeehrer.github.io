Spicio
From idea to second prototype to VC pitch in 8 days.
Project

Working with 3 other students (mechanical, robotics and electrical engineers), I created a robotic, voice-controlled spice rack at the MakeMIT 2016 Hackathon. I utilized Amazon Alexa and Node.js on AWS Lambda to parse responses and send POST requests wirelessly to Particle Photon microcontroller. I also wrote firmware for the microcontroller in C to accept HTTP requests and control a gear motor with encoder. It was my first time working with the Alexa Kills Kit and AWS Lambda and I learned a ton. While the idea for the project started out as a joke, we ended up winning 3rd place out of over 60 teams, as well as a few other awards.

Spicio allows users to simply say things like, "Alexa, pour me 1 teaspoons of cayenne and 2 teaspoons of chili powder." In the future, we want to add functionality for finding recipes and ordering ingredients by voice.

Our team was invited back for the second weekend of hacking where we created a second prototype in 8 hours. Just one day (and about 2 hours of sleep) later, I gave a 5 minute pitch to a panel of VC's as a finalist in AlphaLab Gear's National Hardware Cup in Cambridge. It was an incredible week that further fueled my interest in hackathons and startups.
